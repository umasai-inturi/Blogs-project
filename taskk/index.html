Bhagat singh

Date:1967

Bhagat Singh was a prominent Indian socialist revolutionary whose acts of revolutionary violence against the British in India, particularly the bombing of the Central Legislative Assembly and the assassination of a British police officer, made him a folk hero and martyr. He was hanged at the age of 23 for his revolutionary activities.

Rani Lakshmibai

Date:1967

Rani Lakshmibai's incredible courage, determination, and leadership during the rebellion cemented her place in history. She is remembered as a fearless warrior queen who fought for her kingdom's sovereignty and became a key figure in India's struggle for independence. Her story has been immortalized in folklore, literature, and popular culture, with the Hindi poem "Jhansi Ki Rani" by Subhadra Kumari Chauhan being a particularly famous tribute to her valor.

chandra bose

Date1945

Subhas Chandra Bose, popularly known as Netaji, was a leading figure in India's struggle for independence from British rule. His approach to the freedom movement was revolutionary and differed significantly from the non-violent methods of Mahatma Gandhi.
